Agent OS Community Code of Conduct
Our Pledge
We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Agent OS community pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.
We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.
Agent OS is built on the principle that families should own and govern their own AI infrastructure. This community exists to support that vision through collaboration, mutual respect, and shared learning.

Our Standards
Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment
In Technical Discussions:

Using welcoming and inclusive language
Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
Showing empathy towards other community members
Acknowledging that everyone is learning and growing
Celebrating diverse approaches to implementation
Sharing knowledge freely and patiently

In Governance Discussions:

Engaging in good faith debate about constitutional principles
Respecting that families will configure Agent OS differently
Accepting that multiple perspectives strengthen the project
Focusing on technical merit rather than personal preference
Being open to changing your mind when presented with new information
Recognizing that reasonable people can disagree

In Community Interactions:

Welcoming newcomers warmly
Mentoring those less experienced
Giving credit where credit is due
Being patient with questions, even if repeated
Assuming good intentions unless proven otherwise
Lifting up underrepresented voices

Examples of Unacceptable Behavior
Never Acceptable:

Violence, threats of violence, or violent language directed against another person
Sexist, racist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise discriminatory jokes and language
Posting or displaying sexually explicit or violent material
Posting or threatening to post other people's personally identifying information ("doxing")
Personal insults, particularly those related to gender, sexual orientation, race, religion, or disability
Inappropriate photography or recording
Inappropriate physical contact or unwelcome sexual attention
Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior
Sustained disruption of community events or discussions

Harmful to Project Culture:

Deliberate intimidation or stalking (online or in-person)
Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
Public or private harassment
Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
Gatekeeping or elitism (e.g., "you're not technical enough to contribute")
Dismissing others' concerns or contributions
Pattern of boundary-pushing after being asked to stop
Bad faith arguments or "sea-lioning"
Derailing discussions to push personal agendas
Corporate astroturfing or undisclosed conflicts of interest

Specific to Agent OS:

Attempting to undermine constitutional governance principles
Deliberately sharing insecure configurations without warnings
Encouraging users to bypass security features
Exploiting vulnerabilities before responsible disclosure
Impersonating project maintainers or trusted community members
Spreading misinformation about the project's goals or governance


Our Responsibilities
Community Leaders
Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.
What Community Leaders Will Do

Model the behavior we expect from the community
Respond to reports promptly (within 72 hours)
Investigate thoroughly before taking action
Act fairly and consistently across all community members
Explain decisions when appropriate
Protect reporter privacy at all times
Follow up to ensure resolution
Learn from incidents to improve processes

What Community Leaders Will NOT Do

Act as arbitrary judges of technical merit
Take sides in good faith technical disagreements
Enforce personal preferences as community rules
Make decisions in secret without transparency
Retaliate against good faith reports
Use their position for personal benefit


Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:
Online Spaces:

GitHub repositories (issues, PRs, discussions, wikis)
Discord/Matrix servers (when established)
Community forums and mailing lists
Social media when representing the project
Virtual events and video calls

Physical Spaces:

Conferences and meetups
Workshops and training sessions
Hackathons
Any in-person community gathering

Representation:
This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples include:

Using an official project email address
Posting via official social media accounts
Acting as an appointed representative at events
Speaking on behalf of the project

Note: Personal spaces (your own blog, social media, etc.) are your own, but if you identify yourself as an Agent OS contributor, please consider how your words reflect on the community.

Enforcement
Reporting Violations
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it as soon as possible.
How to Report
Option 1: GitHub (For Public Issues)

Open a private issue in the repository
Tag with "code-of-conduct"
Provide details (see template below)

Option 2: Direct Contact (For Sensitive Issues)

Email: conduct@agentos.org (coming soon)
Subject: "Code of Conduct Report"
Reports sent to this address will only be seen by designated community leaders

Option 3: Anonymous Reporting

Anonymous reporting form (coming soon with community infrastructure)

What to Include
**Date and Time**: When did this occur?

**Location**: Where did this happen? (GitHub issue #123, Discord #general, etc.)

**Individuals Involved**: Who was involved? (Usernames, handles, names)

**Description**: What happened? Please be as detailed as possible.

**Evidence**: Links, screenshots, logs (if available and safe to share)

**Impact**: How did this affect you or others?

**Previous Reports**: Have you reported this person before?

**Desired Outcome**: What would you like to see happen?

**Your Contact Info**: How can we reach you? (Can be anonymous)
What Happens After You Report

Acknowledgment (Within 24-72 hours)

You'll receive confirmation that we received your report
We'll provide a timeline for our response


Investigation (1-7 days depending on complexity)

We'll review all available evidence
We may contact you for additional information
We may contact witnesses or other parties
All investigations are handled with confidentiality


Decision (After investigation completes)

Community leaders will determine if a violation occurred
If yes, we'll determine appropriate consequences
You'll be informed of the outcome (within privacy constraints)


Action (As needed)

Consequences will be enforced
Follow-up will ensure compliance


Appeal (If you disagree with the outcome)

You may appeal decisions within 14 days
Appeals are reviewed by different community leaders
Final decisions will be documented



Confidentiality and Privacy

Reporter Identity: Protected unless you choose to reveal it
Report Contents: Shared only with necessary community leaders
Outcomes: May be shared publicly if serious or precedent-setting, but without identifying details
Records: Maintained for pattern recognition, stored securely


Enforcement Guidelines
Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:
1. Correction
Community Impact: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.
Consequence: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.
Examples:

Using exclusive or insensitive language
Minor disruption of discussions
First-time minor offense

2. Warning
Community Impact: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.
Consequence: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.
Examples:

Repeated inappropriate language after correction
Mild harassment or trolling
Pattern of minor offenses

Duration: Typically 30-90 days
3. Temporary Ban
Community Impact: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.
Consequence: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.
Examples:

Sustained trolling or harassment
Publishing private information without consent
Deliberate intimidation
Repeatedly ignoring community standards

Duration: Typically 3-12 months, with possibility of reinstatement upon demonstrated reform
4. Permanent Ban
Community Impact: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.
Consequence: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
Examples:

Violence or threats of violence
Sustained harassment or stalking
Pattern of serious violations
Doxxing or privacy violations
Sexual harassment
Undermining project security with malicious intent

Duration: Permanent, with no expectation of reinstatement
5. Special Cases
Security Concerns
Violations that pose security risks to users or the project may result in immediate temporary bans while investigation proceeds, followed by appropriate long-term action.
Legal Violations
Illegal activity will be reported to appropriate authorities in addition to community enforcement actions.
Off-Platform Behavior
Serious violations that occur outside community spaces but affect community safety may still result in enforcement actions, particularly if they pose ongoing risk to community members.

Appeal Process
If you believe an enforcement decision was made in error, you may appeal:

Submit Appeal: Within 14 days of decision, email conduct-appeals@agentos.org (coming soon)
Include: Original incident details, decision you're appealing, why you believe it's incorrect
Review: Different community leaders will review (not those involved in original decision)
Decision: Appeal decision is made within 30 days
Final: Appeal decisions are final

Appeals are granted when:

New evidence comes to light
Process wasn't followed properly
Decision was disproportionate to violation
Misunderstanding can be clearly demonstrated


Community Values
Beyond the rules, Agent OS is built on these values:
1. Human Sovereignty
We believe families should control their AI infrastructure. This means:

Respecting diverse approaches to governance
Supporting user autonomy
Enabling, not prescribing, choices

2. Technical Excellence
We strive for high-quality, secure, accessible software:

Rigorous technical standards
Constructive technical debate
Continuous improvement
Learning from mistakes

3. Inclusive Innovation
AI should serve all of humanity:

Welcoming diverse perspectives
Reducing barriers to participation
Amplifying underrepresented voices
Considering global impact

4. Transparency
Open development builds trust:

Public discussions by default
Clear decision-making processes
Honest about limitations
Accountability at all levels

5. Long-Term Thinking
Building for generations, not quarters:

Sustainable development pace
Preventing contributor burnout
Growing the community thoughtfully
Leaving things better than we found them


Positive Community Practices
For Everyone
When You Disagree:

Focus on the idea, not the person
Seek to understand before being understood
Acknowledge valid points in opposing views
Know when to agree to disagree

When You're Right:

Be gracious in victory
Help others understand
Don't say "I told you so"
Share credit generously

When You're Wrong:

Admit it quickly and clearly
Learn from the experience
Thank those who corrected you
Move forward constructively

For Experienced Contributors
Remember:

Everyone was new once
Expertise comes in many forms
Your time spent helping newcomers is an investment
Welcoming culture is everyone's responsibility

Avoid:

"RTFM" responses (link to docs warmly instead)
Sighing at "basic" questions
Assuming malice when ignorance is more likely
Using jargon unnecessarily

For Newcomers
Remember:

Questions are welcome
Making mistakes is how we learn
You bring fresh perspectives
Your feedback on documentation is valuable

Try To:

Search for existing discussions first
Provide context in your questions
Be patient waiting for responses (volunteers!)
Pay it forward by helping others
